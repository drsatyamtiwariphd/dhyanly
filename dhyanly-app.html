Breathing Protocol
AI-prescribed · personalised to your scores
Prescribed For: GAD-2 ≥ 3 · Anxiety
Chandra Bhedana
Left nostril breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system, reduces cortisol, and lowers anxiety markers. Practice daily for 5–10 minutes, ideally in the morning before screens.
1
Prepare
Sit comfortably with your spine upright. Rest your right hand on your right knee. Bring your right thumb to close the right nostril.
2
Inhale Left — 4 counts
Breathe slowly and deeply through your left nostril only. Keep the right nostril gently closed throughout.
3
Hold — 4 counts
Close both nostrils gently. Hold the breath without strain. Keep the body relaxed.
4
Exhale Right — 8 counts
Release the right nostril and exhale slowly and completely. The exhale should be twice as long as the inhale.
5
Repeat × 10 rounds
Continue for 5–10 minutes. After completion, sit quietly for 1 minute and observe the stillness.
